About Neptune Privacy

Neptune Privacy is a quantum-safe Layer 1 blockchain where privacy is automatic, not optional. Every transaction is private by default. No settings to configure, no features to activate, no opt-in required.

We combine three foundational technologies: zk-STARKs, Mutator Sets, and Triton VM, to deliver a chain that is private, programmable, and built to withstand even quantum-level attacks.

Tagline: "Privacy Unchained"

Our Mission

Neptune Privacy exists to solve a fundamental problem in blockchain technology: the tension between decentralization, privacy, and programmability. Most blockchains force you to choose between two of these three.

Neptune gives you all three, and does it for a post-quantum world.

Why Neptune?

Private by Default: Unlike Bitcoin and Ethereum where every transaction is visible, Neptune's transactions are automatically private.

Quantum Safe: While other blockchains rely on elliptic curve cryptography (vulnerable to quantum), Neptune uses hash-based zk-STARKs that are quantum-resistant by design.

Truly Decentralized: Proof of Work consensus with GPU mining. No validators, no staking, no centralized authority.

Programmable Privacy: Smart contracts execute privately via Triton VM, enabling private DEX, confidential lending, anonymous voting, and more.

Community Governed: No CEO. No venture capital. No corporate interests.

Our Story

Neptune Privacy didn't emerge from a whitepaper or a venture capital pitch. It was born from a community that believed deeply in a vision and was willing to act on it.

The Beginning

A group of early NPT investors and supporters had been contributing time, resources, and funding to help the original Neptune (NPT) project grow. The community expanded fast. The technology was groundbreaking: zk-STARKs at the base layer, quantum-resistant privacy, and a programmable architecture that hinted at something truly special.

But one issue became impossible to ignore. The emissions model was not sustainable for a project that needed time to mature. Building a privacy ecosystem requires community, builders, and runway. Not constant sell pressure driven by an inflationary supply.

The Fork Decision

A formal proposal was made to adjust emissions. It was declined. The founders didn't want to change something as fundamental as tokenomics post-launch, a reasonable position, but one the community couldn't align with.

So the idea of a friendly fork was put on the table. Not a rivalry. Not a hostile split. Two paths built on the same technology and vision, diverging toward different economic futures.

NPT — Original Chain

Remains inflationary. Constant new supply entering the market. Sell pressure competes with growth.

XNT — Neptune Privacy

Deflationary with increasing scarcity. Monero-style tail emission keeps the network secure without overwhelming supply.

What We Kept

Because the code is open source, everything people loved about NPT stays intact. The zk-STARK foundation. The quantum-resistant privacy. The programmability. The dream of a rich private smart contract ecosystem. None of that was left behind. Only the economics were refined.

With a sustainable emissions model, XNT can attract far more investors, miners, and builders. That is exactly what is needed to deliver the larger vision originally envisioned: a widely adopted privacy ecosystem capable of supporting tokens, DEXs, and real scalability.

The $45,000 Signal

If anyone was still wondering how serious the XNT team was, the acquisition of the neptune.io domain for $45,000 answered that. Sometimes actions speak louder than words.

Evolution, Not Rebellion

NPT remains an impressive project with world-class privacy technology. The work Alan and Thorkil created is genuinely groundbreaking. Our move isn't rebellion. It's evolution. The economics simply weren't aligned with long-term growth, so the community did what others have done before: preserve the vision, refine the fundamentals, and build a version that can reach its full potential.

XNT keeps everything that made NPT exciting: the zk-STARK foundation, the scalable architecture, the quantum-resistant backbone, the dream of a rich private smart contract ecosystem. But now with emissions that strengthen the chain instead of slowing it down.

Welcome to XNT.

Scalable privacy deserves a chain that isn't weighed down by its own economics. This is where privacy and scalability finally meet. Alan and Thorkil sparked something extraordinary. With XNT, we're carrying that torch forward with healthier fundamentals, a stronger path to adoption, and the momentum to make this the next major privacy ecosystem.
